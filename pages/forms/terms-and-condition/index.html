    The All Africa Music Awards (AFRIMA) is the continent's most prestigious event dedicated to recognizing and celebrating the immense talent and diversity of African music. Established to honor the rich musical heritage of Africa, AFRIMA stands as a beacon of pride for Africa, demonstrating the power of music to unite, inspire, and drive positive change.

    2025 Afrima Call for Entry

    Afrima 2025 Entry Submission Guidlines

    The Guidelines / Requirements for the 2025 All Africa Music Awards Entry Submission

    The All Africa Music Awards, AFRIMA 2025 is officially open for entries from Artistes, Songwriters, Music/Entertainment Journalists, Video Directors, Music Producers, DJs, and Dancers from across the African continent and Diaspora. The AFRIMA awards is celebrating excellence in musical artistry in Africa and the Diaspora, featuring 36 categories across the five regions of Africa and music genre, with a reward of 23.9 carat gold AFRIMA plated trophy.

    The Guidelines / Eligibility / Requirements for the 2025 All Africa Music Awards, AFRIMA Entry Submission

    1. Entries opens from May 10, 2025 and closes on Aug 1st, 2025.
    2. Eligibility: Music professionals of African origin living either on the continent or abroad are eligible.
    3. The award is open to Artistes, Songwriters, Video Directors, Music Producers as well as unrecorded artistes.
    4. Entries submitted through email to any AFRIMA email address WILL NOT be considered.
    5. Artistes / producers / directors / journalists / songwriters must submit their works digitally via AFRIMA website on https://afrima.org/AFRIMA2019,
    6. Stay updated from the AFRIMA social channels Twitter, Instagram @AFRIMAWARDS or Facebook: www.facebook.com/afrima Youtube: www.youtube.com/afrima
    7. It is strongly advised that you submit your work (s) via online submission, the Entry Registration Form must be carefully and accurately completed and submitted including the relevant URL links for the digital version of the artistic work/writing in the space provided.
    8. Forms can be filled in English and French, with Arabic and Portuguese translations available on the form.
    9. African artistes must upload and submit their audio or video entry using download links to YouTube or SoundCloud platforms to qualify for nomination in award categories relevant to their artistic work/works.
    10. Works to be submitted must be produced in the year under review i.e. August 1, 2018 to August 1st, 2019.
    11. For further information, reach out to AFRIMA on social media or other relevant channels of communication.
